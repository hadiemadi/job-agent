How it works

Seven AI agents collaborate every time you tailor a CV.

Recruiter Reads your CV and the job description together. Decides which changes are safe to apply automatically (skills already mentioned but missing from the list, keyword alignment) versus which gaps need your input. Sets the bar for what counts as evidenced vs invented.
CV Writer Applies the approved changes and rewrites the CV. Uses only content that is already in your CV — it never invents facts, adds companies you didn't work at, or inflates titles. Exports to the Executive HTML template and to Word.
Coach Analyses your CV and career direction to suggest the right next role — not just more of the same. Maps those suggestions to real jobs in today's market and traces a path of challenges, skill gaps, and milestones for each direction you could take.
Extractor Parses your CV to identify job titles and field/seniority level. That field label drives the Recruiter's discipline-aware prompts — an RF hardware TPM is reviewed differently from a frontend engineer.
Curator Maintains a per-field knowledge store of skills, keywords, and red-flags. Every time the Researcher surfaces new findings, the Curator merges them — bumping confidence on repeated signals, never touching entries you pinned yourself.
Researcher Fetches up-to-date field conventions from the web — what skills are valued, what keywords appear in real job postings, what red-flags HR teams notice. Currently a stub (no live search yet); the architecture is in place and wired to the Curator.
Input Router Reads anything you type in the "Anything you'd like the AI to know?" box and decides where it belongs: a general instruction (e.g. "prefer one page") flows into every prompt; a field-specific skill claim (e.g. "I know RFIC design") is pinned into the discipline store as a trusted, permanent fact.
